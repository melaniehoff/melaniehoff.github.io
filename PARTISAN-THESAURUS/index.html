Partisan Thesaurus is a pair of thesauruses. The blue thesaurus generates synonyms using a machine learning algorithm that was trained only on a corpus of American liberal-leaning texts. The red thesaurus generates synonyms using a machine learning algorithm that was trained only on a corpus of American conservative-leaning texts.


Our accumulated lived experiences shape who we are and how we perceive the world. Partisan thesaurus focuses specifically on political rhetoric from this corpus of books and transcripts. It suggests that exposure to a narrow set of perspectives alters the associations a person make with any given word.


What makes meaning from a word? It’s static, entry in the dictionary or the associations we make in real-time whenever we hear or use a word. The ideas that a concept like democracy, or immigration are connected to are different for different people.


There’s incredible miscommunication across political and cultural divides, how can we have real understanding if we don’t even agree on what we disagree on?


I hope that partisan Thesaurus can help us to think about the differences in our frames of reference, and find a balanced way to talk about issues.
